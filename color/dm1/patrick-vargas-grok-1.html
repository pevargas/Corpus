This I believe

When I sit down to write about my personal beliefs, I run into writers block. I've attempted to tackle this topic three or four times now and each time I draw a blank. The producers of the This I believe series request the following:

[N]othing less than a statement of your personal beliefs, of the values which rule your thought and action.

My personal beliefs more or less change and develop on a rapid basis. I know who I am, but what do I believe? My focus changes from week to week on what I believe. This week, I believe in extracurriculars. As I'm attempting to find an internship, I meet other people doing the same. These people talk about the previous internships they did when they were freshmen, or they are freshmen looking for an internship. Other people talk about their projects they do in their free time.

While I love computer science, I do participate in extra curriculars. I fully believe this makes me a more well rounded person; a generalist if you will. I play the tuba and bass in a GLBTQ community band. I'm in a gay men's knitting group. I have proper social skills. No, I may not have billion and one open source projects under my belt, but I have lots of friends and meaningful connections. This doesn't mean I'm not a skilled computer science major. It just shows I can handle a multitude of situations.
